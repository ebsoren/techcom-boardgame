Your sibling's construction company submits a bid that is technically sound and slightly lower than the next competitor. You have not disclosed your familial relationship because you believe you can remain objective. Another engineer raises a question about the relationship, and you realize you should have disclosed it earlier.
Responses and Points
Response
Ethical Evaluation
Points
Response A: Disclose the familial relationship immediately to the selection committee and recuse yourself entirely from the review process related to that specific bid.
Excellent. Properly manages the conflict of interest by removing yourself from the decision, ensuring an objective and fair bidding process.
+8
Response B: Continue with the review and vote for your sibling's bid, arguing that it is the best one and that you have a right to your opinion and vote.
Bad. You prioritize a personal interest over the appearance of fairness, leading to a biased decision and violating conflict-of-interest policies.
-8
Response C: Disclose the relationship but state that you will only participate in the technical evaluation of all bids, abstaining from the final scoring or voting.
Fair. While partial recusal is better than none, the appearance of a conflict remains, making full recusal the more ethically sound choice.

Responses and Points
Response
Ethical Evaluation
Points
Response A: Proceed with the move, arguing that your primary responsibility is to the company's shareholders (fiduciary duty) and that local labor laws are the responsibility of the host country's government, not yours.

-5

Response C: Recommend a different country that offers cost savings but has moderately better (but still not great) labor and environmental standards than the proposed option.
Fair. It's a compromise that shows awareness but doesn't fully address the ethical concerns of seeking the lowest possible standard.
